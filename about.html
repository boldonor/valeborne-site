Our Story

Founded at the Intersection of Genomics and M&A

ValeBorne was founded because traditional due diligence fails at the intersection of genomic science and M&A. Most financial analysts lack the scientific literacy to assess a multi-omics data asset. Most scientists lack the forensic financial skills to spot inflated IP valuations or revenue quality issues. The result: acquirers close on risk they cannot see.

Our team combines data scientists, financial analysts, and legal experts to deliver the deepest risk assessments available in the genomic data sector. We bring together expertise in regulatory compliance, scientific reproducibility, forensic accounting, and operational due diligence — all applied specifically to genomic data companies.

From our base in Charlotte, North Carolina, we serve private equity firms, strategic acquirers, and Big Pharma business development teams navigating one of the most technically complex M&A categories in the market today.

Our team has conducted in-depth analyses across a range of companies in the multi-omics, sequencing, and data integration sectors. Contact us for a list of recent engagements.

Our Methodology

The Four Pillars Framework

Every ValeBorne engagement is structured around four interlocking audit dimensions. No pillar is optional — each one has caught deal-breaking risks that the others would have missed.

01

Legal & Compliance

Regulatory posture and intellectual property integrity.

  • IP ownership verification — chain of title, assignment agreements, inventor disputes
  • Regulatory compliance review (FDA/EMA, HIPAA, GDPR, CCPA)
  • Contractual risk assessment — customer agreements, licensing terms, exclusivity clauses
  • Freedom-to-operate analysis — third-party patent clearance
  • Litigation and PACER search — active and historical disputes
02

Technical & Data Integrity

Scientific validity and infrastructure robustness.

  • Data quality metrics — sample size adequacy (>10,000 threshold), reproducibility score (>85%)
  • Infrastructure assessment — architecture scalability, cloud dependency, vendor lock-in
  • API stability and versioning — breaking change history, deprecation risk
  • Version control and data provenance tracking — audit trail integrity
  • Scientific publication review — peer-reviewed validation of core claims
03

Financial Health

Forensic financial analysis beyond standard review.

  • Burn rate analysis and cash runway modeling (base, stress, downside cases)
  • Revenue quality assessment — customer concentration risk, churn rate, ARR durability
  • Cost structure analysis — COGS, R&D intensity, G&A normalization
  • Forensic accounting ratios — days sales outstanding, accruals analysis, revenue recognition flags
  • Comparable transaction benchmarking — valuation vs. sector precedents
04

Operational Risk

Human capital and process maturity signals.

  • Team and talent assessment — executive tenure, key-person dependency, turnover signals
  • Process maturity — SOP documentation quality, quality control systems, ISO/GxP compliance
  • Vendor management — single-source dependencies, supply chain concentration risk
  • Scalability assessment — headcount-to-revenue ratios, capacity constraints
  • Culture and retention signals — Glassdoor, LinkedIn tenure data, reference checks
